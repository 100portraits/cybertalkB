Why Tools Work

The integration of technology in the classroom brings forth numerous benefits and has the potential to greatly enhance the teaching and learning experience. Educators can create interactive, engaging learning environments catering to diverse student needs.

See the tools

Technology offers personalised learning opportunities, allowing students to learn at their own pace and explore areas of interest, thereby increasing motivation and engagement. It promotes active participation and knowledge retention. Additionally, collaborative platforms and communication tools enable students to collaborate on projects and share ideas, fostering teamwork and essential social skills. The integration of technology in the classroom holds great potential to transform traditional teaching practices and create student-centred, dynamic learning environments that better prepare students for the challenges of the information and technology-driven society.

"Teachers were the centre, while students were the silent, passive objects. This concept has changed today: students are the centre of the teaching and learning process."

Hassel, B. C., & Hassel, E. A. (2011)

In a study “Improving Teachers’ Professionalism through Materials Development, Information Technology, and Classroom Action Research.” (Sukarni et al., 2009), it was noticed that teachers’ collaboration on creating teaching materials leads to their increased confidence, enhanced level of competency, and willingness to improve professionally.

In the materials that they made, use of methods such as gamification (matching pictures with the paragraphs, answering questions related to the texts, crossword puzzle) and story-telling (writing their own narrative text based on the steps and scaffold given and learnt) was met with positive feedback from students, saying that it is more understandable and fun.
